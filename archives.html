Editor's Picks

A Game of Kings

On the board of eight by eight
An army prepares
For war, intimidating the opponent
With steadfast, iron glares

When comes the pawn, an infantryman
Marking the beginning
Of a battle of the most glorious type
Of sacrifice for the king

Soon blood does stain the battlefield
The objective ever clear
To scare the king out of his wits
To trap the king with fear

Alas! Alas! Our king is dead
Bested, that he was
From the few survivors a groan does rise
They fought for an empty cause

The Roundabout Race

The Cat and The Mouse were dashing around the house
In a frenzied sort of race
The Mouse was scared to his bones
You should have seen his face

Round and round they went about
The circuit that was home
Jolting here and bolting there
Everywhere the mouse did roam!

So as the two did swerve into
The peaceful nursery
A cry of panic, of utter distress
Came from the way of the startled baby

The mouse did know that if they found him
It would be certain doom
The mouse was wise so he let his eyes
Guide him to his room

Life Cycle of a Bubble

A floating orb, a glittering sphere
Bouncing between there and here
Higher and lower he does go about
Drifting everywhere, what is its route?

The glossy ball goes up and down
And all around the town
Hopping and bopping it's going to go places
Meeting new people, greeting new faces!

Now gaining altitude it did go
Soaring above the trees
But he will soon come to know
That that too has its fees

Cruising o'er the pinpoint people
His story shan't end with a drop
Instead, while rounding the tall church steeple
He ends with a petty pop!



Quarantine Diaries

A Sunday Night in
Quarantine

Near the buzz of the TV
As happy as one could be
On a night in Quarantine

When the beast is lurking
Invisible and bold
Inside, me and my family
Take shelter from the cold

Why do I stare away from
The daunting outside world
Perhaps to keep myself from
The disaster that's unfurled

Curled up with my family
Near the buzz of the TV
I yearn to be free

Break for Busy Bees

Marvelous Monday Madness
Is now alll but gone
Silence does take its place
Come the first rays of dawn
Accompanied by
A symphony of yawns

Silent City

What is a city without its people
Is what I wonder
As I stare out my window
Sans screams
Sans shrieks
Sans citizens
Its true colors it cannot show

When the green is replaced by white
Of hospitals
Of fear
Then in the parks
Not chirps
But silence do you hear



Miscellaneous Poetry

The Snowstorm

We trek and trek through the biting snow
Up and up and up we go
Each snowflake biting like a knife
Face frozen cold
But...
It's inevitable, a part of life

What idiocy is "Winter Wonderland"
I'd rather be on the beach
In the sand

Tree (Haiku)

The tree is standing
So majestic and regal
Thunk! the tree is dead

Seed or Tree

What came first
The seed or the tree?
But a circle has no start
So how can such an answer be?
Yet a circle is drawn starting somewhere
So I must disagree
And while the latter is true
When a circle is drawn
The start you cannot see


My Brother

In every land place or time
Every location, every clime
My brother has been with me

Every Everest turned a mound
When I heard the comforting sound
Of my brother's footsteps inching towards me

Though younger he may be
He forever motivates me
When all hope is lost

Consoling and controlling me
Is harder than it looks, you see
Yet my brother's always there

So even on the wildest rides
My brother's always by my side

The Grumblings of a
Sun Lover

Oh! How I long for the days of summer
When the days are nice and long
When the little birds, in their trees
Sing a long forgotten song
Carried by the warm, mellow breeze
Cleansed by April showers
Every breath full of laughter and ease
Oh! How I long for the days of summer

Yet now we're stuck with winter
Where the lights go out snip-snap
Before we know it-the sky is pitch black

When comes the biting cold
Omnipresent, everywhere
Everyone in its hold
For winter is its lair
Oh! Why must it be winter?

Achilles' Heel

You may wear a mask to conceal yourself
Yet I can still see your face
For one's true colors shine behind the cloth
Cotton, wool and lace

You may be hardened like steel
But even that does melt
For when an arrow struck Achilles' heel
He fell and others knelt

Don't keep your quiver too close to you
For then it shall stain
For longing and hopefulness
Yet with agony and pain


This Dawn (A Cywydd)

Can you taste the ocean spray
mingled with the ar today?

Do you see the willows dance
Swaying over other plants?

Sun lights up the blazing sky
Reds and yellows, my oh my

Forever it will live on
I will remember this dawn
